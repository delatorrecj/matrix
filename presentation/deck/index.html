The Problem

ASEAN cities build infrastructure blind.

  • Static feasibility studies age the day they're filed. A 2022 traffic count cannot predict how a 2026 mall reshapes every household within 3 km, or which jeepney route, vendor corner, or flood corridor it disrupts.
  • Cross-domain impacts are judged in silos. Environmental, transport, economic, and social reviews live in different offices, and reach the same desk only after the project is approved.
  • The tools that could help need specialists. Tools like Vissim, Aimsun, CityEngine, and Replica cannot take "what happens if we build a 3,000-seat school here?" from a planner with no modeling background.
Documented planning-failure pattern across ASEAN suburban cities (illustrative: Montalbo / UP SURP; JICA; TRID; ICLEI Iloilo Roadmap).

The Solution

Drop a project on your city's simulator.
Get a calibrated 5-dimension answer in 90 seconds.

  • Natural language or a map drop like "add a 3-storey school in Mandurriao."
  • Thousands of agent simulations run against a simulator of the city.
  • Five scored impact dimensions, each with an explicit confidence interval.
  • Watch it play out on the map; read a cited narrative; export a report.

The honest difference

MATRIX reports −₱8M to −₱14M over 5 yrs (Medium confidence) which is a calibrated range, rather than a false-precise "−₱11.3M." It says where it's sure and where it's estimating.

Appendix — Q&A Defense

How we know it's right, and where it isn't (yet).

Glass-box methodology

Every number carries equation_id + input_dataset_ids + a computed confidence, and must resolve in the Inspect drawer. The LLM narrates and cites — it never originates a number. Rule: no working Inspect → not done.

Confidence rubric

H/M/L computed from data vintage × spatial coverage × method maturity × validation status; the worst factor caps the tier. Low-data dimensions render "directional only," never a precise number.

Validation roadmap (planned, not shipped)

RMSE vs the Calderon 2014 Iloilo BRT model; IoU back-test vs the 2024 Iloilo flood (Sentinel-1 GFM); mode-share within ±3% of ground truth (enforced today by the bias auditor).

Honest constraints

90 s holds warm/single-user (cold + multi-user need queueing); mode-share is literature-derived (Medium) pending a live survey; FOI data (LTFRB VI) may lag submission — open substitutes cover the build.
